Trouble and anguish have come upon me, _Yet_ Your commandments are my delight.

[Psalm 119:143](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?version=NASB&search=Psalm%20119:143 "Psalm 119:143") [NASB](https://www.biblegateway.com/versions/index.php?action=getVersionInfo&vid=49 "New American Standard Bible")

  

[Proverbs 22:6](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Proverbs+22%3A6&version=NASB)

  

**Train** **up** **a** **child** in the way he should go, Even when he is old he will not depart from it.

  

[Luke 22:19](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=Luke+22:19&version=NASB)\-20

And when He had taken _some_ bread _and_ given thanks, He broke it and gave it to them, saying, “**This** is My body which is given for you; **do** **this** **in** **remembrance** of Me.”

20 And in the same way _He took_ the cup after they had eaten, saying, “This cup which is poured out for you is the new covenant in My blood.

  

  

Sometimes we need to remember. Sometimes we need the familiar.

  

The very act of “doing” something can often help us remember, and then we find comfort in that memory.

  

The psalmists remembrance that Gods commandments are his delight.. whenever in times of trouble we can find ourselves in HIM. 

  

  

As defined on Wiki, muscle memory is:

\- a form of procedural memory that involves consolidating a specific motor task into memory through repetition. When a movement is repeated over time, a long-term muscle memory is created for that task, eventually allowing it to be performed without conscious effort.

  

Muscle memory is simply the process by which we consolidate a specific motor task into memory through repetition. Whatever the habit or movement worked on becomes so natural, it doesn’t need much conscious effort.

  

In karate, as with just about any sport, muscle memory is key. you start doing something correctly and keep doing it correctly and then later,  you can find peace in the muscle memory. You can concentrate on learning the new because your muscles remember how to do the parts you already know.

  

Faith is like that. WE have spiritual muscles, Trust, and obey. When we trust him it’s a spiritual muscle, when we obey what He says thats a spiritual muscle. If we aren’t careful we can exercise these muscles incorrectly, we cause hurt and harm when we do that. If we trust when we should be obeying then we will be out of step.

  

Imagine a platoon of soldiers or a marching band, if one person is out of step it can mess up the whole formation. One person with the wrong muscle memory can cause the WHOLE to fail. Thats important.

  

The more we give, pray, worship and serve, the stronger we will become in those areas. Sometimes I think what is the point? But framing routine practice with the concept of muscle memory, I am encouraged to keep up my spiritual training.

  

Sometimes we are called to a place for a time. I know what God starts never ends, however, sometimes we are called to move on, and in that case if we don’t move to where we are now called, we will be out of step. our muscle memory will keep working, but we will be without the initiation of the Spirit. We will appear OK but we will slowly descend into disrepair as we can no longer eat at the table where we find ourselves.  It’s often sad, sometimes heartbreaking, but it should be a time of joy and praise. 

  

I have been there, I find myself constantly praying now, God am I still where you want me to be? I don’t want to be stuck in muscle memory and have no life, no vitality, on vigor. 

  

We do the things we do and it draws us closer to God, our spiritual body learns how to function. When I was little we went to the methodist church, and at that time, at least, there was a lot of standing up here, sitting down then, saying the right thing at the right time, all as part of the service. in many ways it’s beautiful and in some ways it can be deceptive, you fall into the habit, the muscle memory, of doing the right things at the right times and it appears that you are good to go.  Then someone throws in a monkey-wrench. Someone speaks up out of turn, and the whole thing comes crashing down! the entire band trips over that one person. the entire column of soldiers topples because one person had a different drummer. 

  

Sometimes that’s how God shakes things up. Sometimes that’s how God points out to the person that they are really called elsewhere. 

  

There is no reason to fear muscle memory, it’s a good thing it can be a comfort. Sometimes when we are in times of peril we find ourselves”going through the motions” not because we have to, but because we find peace and comfort there. 

  

Just be sure to listen to what God is telling you. Be sure to pay attention to what is around you. The last thing you want to do is cause the entire company to fail, so… pay attention to what God is telling you. Trust Him with all your heart, lean not to your own understanding in all your ways acknowledge Him and he SHALL direct your paths. 

  

Trust him and then obey his direction.

  

Why am I sharing this today? I have no clue… like I said I pray every week, God am I still doing what you want me to do? I believe I am. If not.. then it will be a scary exciting time… but I think I still am. I know how God has called me in my spirit to be who and what I am where I am to reach a certain group of people. to reach out to those who might not otherwise find church. To tell those who may be looked down upon by the religious that God loves them, no matter what. 

  

Out spiritual muscle memory isn’t a bad thing, but we have to make sure that we train our muscles the way they should go… (learn it correctly) and practice correctly.  If you have bad form when you start learning you will have to unlearn everything you learned in order to relearn the correct form.

  

Don’t waste time training incorrectly. Train correctly. If you don’t know what is correct, then ask God, He will direct you, he will teach you.

  

In Tang Soo Do, if you ask the master how to do something, he will show you, step by step, happily, (provided you are ready for that) but he expects you to listen and do your best to imitate him. If he shows you and you simply ignore him, then.. well then you will find yourself doing pushups… but if it continues you will find yourself outside alone.  If you are going to ask the master how to do something, then be prepared to DO IT the way you are shown. 

  

When we ask God how and he shows us… be prepared to do it THAT way. If we ask God Where, then be prepared to GO. Don’t be afraid to try and fail. Be afraid of not trying.

  

This week, remember what God has shown you, remember how he has taught you, remember the muscle memory and put it to work where and when and how God directs you!
