# Romans 12 New King James Version (NKJV)

### Living Sacrifices to God

12 I beseech you therefore, brethren, by the mercies of God, that you present your bodies a living sacrifice, holy, acceptable to God, _which is_ your reasonable service. 2 And do not be conformed to this world, but be transformed by the renewing of your mind, that you may prove what _is_ that good and acceptable and perfect will of God.

### Serve God with Spiritual Gifts

3 For I say, through the grace given to me, to everyone who is among you, not to think _of himself_ more highly than he ought to think, but to think soberly, as God has dealt to each one a measure of faith. 4 For as we have many members in one body, but all the members do not have the same function, 5 so we, _being_ many, are one body in Christ, and individually members of one another. 6 Having then gifts differing according to the grace that is given to us, _let us use them:_ if prophecy, _let us prophesy_ in proportion to our faith;7 or ministry, _let us use it_ in _our_ ministering; he who teaches, in teaching; 8 he who exhorts, in exhortation; he who gives, with liberality; he who leads, with diligence; he who shows mercy, with cheerfulness.

### Behave Like a Christian

9 _Let_ love _be_ without hypocrisy. Abhor what is evil. Cling to what is good. 10 _Be_ kindly affectionate to one another with brotherly love, in honor giving preference to one another;11 not lagging in diligence, fervent in spirit, serving the Lord; 12 rejoicing in hope, patient in tribulation, continuing steadfastly in prayer; 13 distributing to the needs of the saints, given to hospitality.

14 Bless those who persecute you; bless and do not curse. 15 Rejoice with those who rejoice, and weep with those who weep. 16 Be of the same mind toward one another. Do not set your mind on high things, but associate with the humble. Do not be wise in your own opinion.

17 Repay no one evil for evil. Have regard for good things in the sight of all men. 18 If it is possible, as much as depends on you, live peaceably with all men. 19 Beloved, do not avenge yourselves, but _rather_ give place to wrath; for it is written, “Vengeance _is_ Mine, I will repay,”\[[a](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=romans+12&version=NKJV#fen-NKJV-28265a "See footnote a")\] says the Lord. 20 Therefore

“If your enemy is hungry, feed him;
If he is thirsty, give him a drink;
For in so doing you will heap coals of fire on his head.”\[[b](https://www.biblegateway.com/passage/?search=romans+12&version=NKJV#fen-NKJV-28266b "See footnote b")\]

21 Do not be overcome by evil, but overcome evil with good.

There are a wide variety of stories here.. so. how do any of these tie in with Romans Chapter 12?

Well let me try to explain... Careful now, this is where the wheels of traditionalism might start slipping.

The first part of chapter 12 is written to believers, clearly.  "I beseech you therefore, brethren..."  he wants us to present ourselves as a living sacrifice.. a sacrifice is something given and not taken back.. The writer here clearly wants us to give ourselves over to God and not take our own lives back into our own hands.

He also wants us to be "transformed by the renewing of our minds" so that we can prove (I'll come back to that word in a sec. ) what is the good, acceptable and perfect Will of God.

To PROVE something.. the prove has two definitions in the Google online dictionary at any rate. the first is to "demonstrate the truth or existence of (something) by evidence or argument." and the second is "(of bread dough) become aerated by the action of yeast; rise." To Prove what is the good, acceptable, and perfect will of God means that we are not only giving evidence that God's Will exists.. but by our very actions we are leavening the whole. We are bringing lightness and air into situations and relationships.

The writer then tells us not to think too highly of ourselves... after all the eye couldn't see much if the foot couldn't move the body.  Sometimes, if we aren't careful we will think we are more important than we really are. Face it.. we can exist without a lot of body parts.. but to be complete we have to be "all there"  He also wants us to "behave like Christians"  Here is where the path gets a little jumbled...

What is a Christian and how do I behave like one?   Well if you ask about 3/4 of the population what a Christian is they will tell you someone who goes to Church... ask them how a Christian behaves and you are likely to have burning ears by the time they are done describing how a "christian" acted towards them.

So let's assume that this means not only behave like Christians to other Christians.. but to the world as well. The gifts of the spirit and the callings of the church are put together here... they really are pretty much the same..  "6 Having then gifts differing according to the grace that is given to us, _let us use them:_ if prophecy, _let us prophesy_ in proportion to our faith;7 or ministry, _let us use it_ in _our_ ministering; he who teaches, in teaching; 8 he who exhorts, in exhortation; he who gives, with liberality; he who leads, with diligence; he who shows mercy, with cheerfulness."

Not much to be added there.. if you have gifts and callings use them happily in service to the church and to others. to Others? really? the Bible does say that the gifts and the callings are without repentance.. that means that there are parts of the body that are still completely immersed in the world. People who are connected to us, as Christians who don't know Christ...

Verses 9-21 tell us how to behave as Christians..  get ready now, this might rock your boat.  When we as the church feel persecuted or used... how do we react?  We are supposed to bless those who persecute us. If someone uses us we are to walk the extra mile with them. Think about things we see in the world today, across the nation there are people trying to get gay marriage.  We as Christians are not to oppose them. (See I told you ... rock rock rock)  The businesses that refuse to serve on the grounds that it's against their "moral" code and goes against their beliefs.. haven't read Romans 12. (that said I think that any business in a free market has the right to simply say no I don't want you as my customer.., that is a business right.. but has nothing to do with morals) According to Romans 12 we are to bless those who would persecute us. Face it most of these bakeries or restaurants have been targeted because someone "knew" they would refuse.

According to Romans 12, and to the way that Jesus served and told us to serve all men (and women) the response should have been. I am a Christian, I don't think that Gay Marriage is what God had in mind when He created marriage, but I am going to make you the best wedding cake you have ever had and I'm going to make it with all the love that Jesus has poured out on me.  Now one of two things would happen here.. the people asking and expecting to be refused would decide they really don't want a cake made with the love of Jesus and would simply leave.. or they would be so spoken to that it would speak to them for the rest of their life. a seed would be planted.. someone else might have to water it, but a seed would be there none-the-less.

Think about it.. what if instead of protesting that we are being persecuted, we as Christians started Blessing every person who persecutes us?  Oh I know this would be HARD so hard.. but think what would happen?

This would lift Jesus... Jesus said, if I be lifted up I shall draw all men!  Think of it. how high would it lift Christ if every single Christian were to bless those they feel are persecuting or cursing them?

I'm telling you,  we don't have to agree with homosexuality, but we don't have the right to judge anyone either, that's not our job. Jesus made that very clear.  See if I love someone to the point that they ask me what I think about a topic, homosexuality, gay marriage, whatever, then they will hear what I say.  Otherwise, I am a clanging brass and a tinkling cymbal, something to be tuned out.  Wait a second, didn't Paul say something like that in 2 Corinthians?   Yeah I think he did.

So folks, I'm telling you.. this throws out the judgementalism  of the church. You can't judge and love too. Unless you are the Son of God, He has that capacity, but we as humans.. not so much. You can't say "we don't do things like that around here..." and expect the Spirit of God to be able to flow. We have to let go of these things which are keeping us hung up and simply let God take us where he wants.  What about Holiness, you say?  Romans 12 the first few verses covers that.. Be the Christian God is calling you to be, don't waver don't falter, don't go easy on yourself... notice, I said on yourself.. your whole Christian walk, as far as you are concerned is between you and your savior, not between you and your spouse, your family, your church or your neighbors.  The fruit of being the kind of Christian we need to be will come out in how we love others.

Now I know.. I said some things here that will anger some people if they ever hear it.. good!

Let them be angry, let them take it to the Lord in prayer and see what He has to say about it.
