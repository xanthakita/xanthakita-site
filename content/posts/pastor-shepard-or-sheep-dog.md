I have often heard the pastor of a church referred to as the shepherd of the flock. I have one question, if that is the case then where does that leave Jesus? He said in John 10 "11 “I am the good shepherd. The good shepherd gives His life for the sheep. 12 But a hireling, _he who is_ not the shepherd, one who does not own the sheep, sees the wolf coming and leaves the sheep and flees; and the wolf catches the sheep and scatters them. 13 The hireling flees because he is a hireling and does not care about the sheep.14 I am the good shepherd; and I know My _sheep,_ and am known by My own. 15 As the Father knows Me, even so I know the Father; and I lay down My life for the sheep. 16 And other sheep I have which are not of this fold; them also I must bring, and they will hear My voice; and there will be one flock _and_ one shepherd.

Now if Jesus is the Good shepherd, then how can the pastor be the shepherd of the flock... if the pastor is any kind of shepherd, he is a hireling... and Jesus didn't think very highly of them.

I say that the pastor may not be a sheep, but more of a sheep dog... not the kind that are used in the west that drive the sheep and nip and control them, but the kind that are used in the east... sheepdogs that guard the sheep, usually as part of the herd, they sleep with the sheep, eat with the sheep, grow up with the sheep. They may even think they are sheep, however, when the thief comes, the pastor shows that he has teeth.

The Great Pyrenees Dog is a perfect example:

![](/posts/pastor-shepard-or-sheep-dog/1.jpg)

These dogs are raised as part of the flock. They love the sheep they are with, yet if a wolf shows up, they are terrible in their response, they will fight to the death to protect the sheep.

This is what a pastor is supposed to be, in my opinion. Not the one that sets the direction, that is the Good Shepherd's job, not the one that sets the pace, or sets anything.. but the one that follows the shepherd closely watching out that none of the sheep come into danger.

I heard a story about a young man (several years ago) who was in a church, loving God, learning about the Bible... He had some folks contact him and tell him they would love it if he had a Bible study... he didn't try it. didn't push it, didn't look for it, he didn't even do it. He received a call from his senior pastor saying, "If you lead this Bible study, I will destroy you... with the love of God."

Uhm... last time I looked the love of God doesn't destroy. That love can tear down to rebuild. It will break down high places.. but it doesn't destroy the pure of heart.  A Pastor that says this is like a thief.. he has taken the flock as his own, and not as a guardian of the Lord's flock.

I don't know what happened with this pastor, but I know that he will be held accountable for this. I don't want to be held accountable like that... when a sheep dog tries to destroy or savage a sheep the only solution is to put the dog down.

God help us if we are ever in a position where we want to destroy a lamb instead of protect it. Jesus said that it would be better to have a mill-stone tied around your neck and have it thrown into the sea than to harm one of these little ones...

If you are called to be a pastor remember we are here to guard, to protect, to assist the shepherd, not to be the leader, not to set the course, not to set the vision. Our job is to be a part of the flock, to protect the flock and to teach the young how to follow the master.

Disney had a cute short animation years ago called Lambert the Sheepish Lion. He fits the image perfectly (only he's a lion instead of a dog.)
